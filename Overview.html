Overview

A new invisible enemy, only 30kb in size, has emerged from Wuhan, China and is on a killing spree around the world: 2019-nCoV, the Novel Coronavirus! The first case in Nepal was confirmed on 23 January 2020 when a 31-year-old student, who had returned to Kathmandu from Wuhan on 9 January, tested positive for the disease. It was also the first recorded case of COVID-19 in South Asia. Between January and March, Nepal took steps to prevent a widespread outbreak of the disease while preparing for it by procuring essential supplies, equipment and medicine, upgrading health infrastructure, training medical personnel, and spreading public awareness. The first case of local transmission was confirmed on 4 April in Kailali District. The first death occurred on 14 May. A country-wide lockdown came into effect on 24 March 2020, and ended on 21 July 2020. As of 16 September 2020, the Ministry of Health and Population (MoHP) has confirmed a total of 58,327 cases, 41,706 recoveries, and 379 deaths in the country.

This dashboard mainly focuses on different styles of data visualization related to COVID19 Nepal. It is divided into two parts i.e Districtwise & Provincewise. Both contains web map, dynamic map created in arcmap software & Race bar graph created using fluorish studio. Also as an additional information, Blood database has been added into the top of the page which you can visit through this link too where you can get blood databse of people of different blood group living all over Nepal
